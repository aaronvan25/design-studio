# Anti-Patterns — What to Avoid

## Visual Anti-Patterns

- Rounded-corner card UIs (use sharp corners by default)
- Gradient buttons (use inverted solid buttons: fg background, bg text)
- Emoji in UI (never use emoji in professional interfaces)
- Playful color palettes (use two-tone + opacity hierarchy)
- Material Design defaults (elevated cards, FABs, snackbars)
- Bootstrap defaults (btn-primary, card with shadow, container-fluid)
- Drop shadows for depth (use zone coloring and opacity layers instead)
- Skeleton loaders (use staggered fade-ins or nothing)
- Toast notifications with icons (use timeline nodes or inline status)
- "Friendly" illustration styles (no Undraw, no Humaaans)
- Generic SaaS aesthetics (white cards on gray bg, rounded everything)
- Conic-gradient donut charts (too generic)
- Star ratings with yellow stars (too playful)

## Layout Anti-Patterns

- Single scrolling column of cards (use zoned layouts)
- Generic sidebar + main content + no atmosphere
- Everything centered with uniform spacing
- Content cut off by the viewport (always check all content fits)
- Identical 3-column layouts repeated across different projects

## Typography Anti-Patterns

- System sans-serif for everything (always pick fonts with personality)
- All text at the same size/weight (use dramatic hierarchy)
- Uppercase everywhere (mix cases intentionally)
- No letter-spacing variation (tight on large, wide on small labels)
- Comic Sans, Papyrus, or other "fun" fonts

## Animation Anti-Patterns

- Animation for animation's sake (every movement needs purpose)
- Too many competing animations (one or two focal points, not ten)
- Bouncing, sliding, or popping transitions (breathe, pulse, fade)
- Decorative canvas effects that add no meaning
- Particles/shaders just to look cool when they don't serve the content
- Inconsistent animation timing across a page

## Color Anti-Patterns

- Neon-everything "gamer" aesthetic on serious subjects
- Light-mode newspaper treatments that break a dark theme
- More than 2-3 colors doing the work (use opacity variations instead)
- Status colors used for decoration (reserve for severity only)

## Content Anti-Patterns

- "Good morning, [Name]! 👋" greetings
- "Here's what's happening today" generic copy
- "Upgrade to Pro! 🚀" upsell cards
- "View All →" generic buttons
- Placeholder text ("Lorem ipsum") in final designs
- "+$2,341 ✅ Completed" with emoji status indicators

## The Litmus Test

Ask: "Does this look like it was designed by a human with strong opinions, or generated by a component library?" If the answer is the latter, start over.
